About

My interests lie in data. Quantifying it, modeling it, building machine learning systems around it.

Right now that covers propellant chemistry for spacecraft thrusters, mass spectrometry data in proteomics, and human movement. Different fields, same core work: take real-world measurements and turn them into models that mean something. SVMs, random forests, XGBoost, LSTMs, depending on the problem.

I care about models that can explain their own answers. A number without a reason behind it isn't useful to me.

I'm a CS student at Cornell. Data structures, linear algebra, discrete math, computer vision. I also went through Y Combinator's Startup School, thinking about how research turns into something people actually use.

Outside of research, I swim competitively, wingfoil, play volleyball, and play chess. I've also spent time organizing a hackathon for a few hundred high schoolers.
