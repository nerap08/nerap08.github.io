Elementary School

Before middle school even began, I was ignited by a passion for coding. On a memorable trip to Peru, our tour guide provided my family with an exceptionally personalized experience. In appreciation of his efforts, I developed a website for him, ensuring he could connect with more customers and grow his venture.

Middle School

During my middle school years in Italy, my friends and I ventured into competitive programming. Our dedication bore fruit as we clinched 1st place in MBIT for middle school teams in both 7th and 8th grades. Furthermore, representing my school, I participated in a European math competition, marking my growing interest in logical and analytical challenges.

Freshman Year

My high school journey began with a deep dive into the study of balance. Being an ardent fan of wing foiling, I noticed the challenges many faced in maintaining balance. This observation led me to design a waterproof device that not only measured and recorded tilt but also used LED lights to indicate the incline of the wing foil. Using this data, I developed regression models predicting balance patterns based on previous data sets.

Sophomore Year

Sophomore year marked an expansion of my coding interests as my friends and I reimagined the classic board game Battleship into a video game. Moreover, I co-founded a game development club, gathering a consistent group of 5-10 students weekly. Together, we delved into the world of Unity, equipping them with the skills to bring their imaginations to life.

Summer before Junior Year

During the summer before my junior year, I interned at a startup called Verihire. Here, I learned to code AI bots using the chat.gpt's API. I also acquired skills in React, creating dynamic applications for user input. In addition, I gained an understanding of how role management functions in companies and the responsibilities individuals in these roles need to undertake.

Junior Year

By my junior year, I stepped up as the president of the Hack Club, one of the most significant clubs in our school. My leadership role extended as I spearheaded the organization of a Hackathon, which can be explored here. Beyond club activities, I pursued a research project exploring the influence of muscle memory and new surroundings on an individual's ability to balance. My aim was to discern strategies to enhance balance, especially for those with health concerns.
